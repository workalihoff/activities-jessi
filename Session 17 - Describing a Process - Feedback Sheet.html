What you did well: You described a real, technical banking process (ACH transfers) in English under live pressure — and you got the sequence right. What we're working on is the grammatical packaging around that content.
4 Patterns to work on
1
Missing auxiliary verbs ★ Priority
In English, passive and progressive constructions need an auxiliary verb (is / are / was). You can't use the -ing form or past participle alone.
✗"The payment sent to the ALTO app."
✓"The payment is sent to the ALTO app."
✗"This app doing your verification."
✓"The app performs the verification."
✗"The regulator sending the payment."
✓"The regulator sends the payment."
2
3rd person -s dropping
When the subject is he / she / it / the team / the bank — every active verb in the sequence needs -s, not just the first one.
✗"The client input the payment."
✓"The client enters the payment."
✗"This bank release the funds."
✓"The bank releases the funds."
✗"I handling ACH transfer process."
✓"I handle the ACH transfer process."
3
"This" vs. "The"
Use "this" only when you introduce something new or point to something. Once it's already in the conversation, switch to "the."
✗"This app doing your verification."
✓"The app performs the verification."
✗"This payment are sending to the regulator."
✓"The payment is sent to the regulator."
✗"This bank release the funds."
✓"The bank releases the funds."
4
Word form under pressure
When speaking fast, noun and verb forms can get switched. Pause to check: do you need a noun or a verb here?
✗"Sometimes when the payment failure…"
✓"Sometimes when the payment fails…"
✗"I explain the step-by-step of the ACH…"
✓"Let me walk you through the process step by step."
✗"outgoing ACH" (when meaning incoming)
✓Outgoing = money leaving the bank. Incoming = money arriving.
📌 The two-pattern model for describing any process
Active step
Subject + verb-s
"The client enters the payment in the online banking portal."
Passive step
Subject + is/are + past participle
"The payment is sent to the ALTO app for verification."
💬
Quick vocabulary fix: When referring to Citi's clients in English, use "our account holders" or "our clients" — not just "the client" (which is ambiguous). Example: "Our account holders enter the payment in the online banking portal."
